Tips for the Perfect Logo for Your Brand

How to create an exciting logo for yourself in just a few minutes?

The logo is a symbol or emblem that acts as a trademark or a means of identifying a company or organisation. Therefore, logos are unique and represent the business, it’s goals, and it’s motivation.

A logo provides an insight into your business, or it’s functioning. These days the task of creating a logo is more entrusted in the hands of graphic designers.

But if you know what you want to sell through your logo, it’s best to do it your way.

This will not only be cost and time effective in causing you to spend less in conveying your requirements. But it will also help you stick to the motto and give importance to only those factors which you feel significant while designing your logo.

Here are six things you need to keep in mind while designing a logo:

1. What is business all about?

A symbol is used for representation and identification of an entity and associates with it. However, it’s never said that a logo should tell or hint about the functions of a business.

It’s all upon if you want to align the business Idea with its symbol. But usually, it is always expected to enlighten the values and goals of the organization and desire for your logo. 

2. Simple as it gets!!

Do not make your logo so complicated that it would make it too difficult to replicate and understand. It should be as simple to identify and comply with an extent to the ability. 

3. All Colors do not do equally!

Specific colours influence sales differently. Let’s say food sales influenced by red and yellow colour. Therefore, many food companies have a red and yellow coloured logo. Colours are interpreted and communicated more rapidly and efficiently.

Therefore, the colour of the logo makes it the most researched part of it.

4. Tagline required?

A slogan can be used to accompany a logo if required. A three-seven words tagline is usually beautiful to create a brand image and standing. If it’s influential, inspiring it will add value to your logo. And the logo can be used singly as well.

Advertising through slogans has created a massive revolution, and promotional taglines have the potential to cause a change. 

5. A logo can be anything…
  • It’s not required for a logo to represent what you do.
  • So many logos use the initials of a name.
  • Some logo has hidden meanings which can’t be understood unless told.
  • Some logos tell a story and encourage you to take a step forward.
  • Some logos have a story of their own
  • A few logos are just the name, which is enough to take the legacy ahead.
  • It can also be your lucky charm if you believe in superstitions.
