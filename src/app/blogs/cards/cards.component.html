Why Report Card’s Design is Important?

Report cards record a student's academic and co-curricular performance in a structured and systematic manner. The report card conveys the progressive development of the student and is valued lifelong.

They carry the student's scores and information regarding their academic progress. They are retained lifelong as a reliable document assisting a student in his successive and subsequent accomplishments, qualifications and evaluation.

A report card consists of descriptors and narratives.

Descriptors usually include different sets of distinct degrees of performance, which can be used to describe students.

Narrative comprises the comments of teachers. It is available in paragraphs where they attempt at summarizing the performance, the need and scope of improvement and a complete analysis of the person, his strengths, shortcomings and interests.

Here are a few things required to be able to make a report card. 

1) Components of a report card

A report card includes parents’ information and students’ profile and grades and achievements during the semester year.

  • First and last name
  • Mailing address and email id
  • The contact number of parents

For students information

  • Name and grade of the student
  • The score of the student in various subjects, along with comments and remarks. They do contain practical information like the mode of transportation.
  • School contact information and address

2) Sizes adopted

The education system and board changes, the parameters also change. But now many follow the same trend and print grades on standard printer paper.

3) What report cards made of?

Report cards usually are found to be formed from cardboard, card stock paper or some heavy paper.

Report cards are meant to be kept and stored and saved for a long. It would be futile to have them if their durability is less. They should be made of heavy paper material so that they can be secured for eternity. They hold much importance to students and should look valuable, too, in terms of its longevity.

4) Report card designing

Report cards are official and formal documents. There should be a minimal designing approach to their generation. Colours and fonts should be as professional as it seems. The school name, logo, images, and extraordinary feats achieved are usually seen on report cards.

The details of scores and other information should be evident and intelligible so that parents don't find it challenging to comprehend.
