Certificates are issued to acknowledge, appreciate and recognize the contribution of a member or a participant. Certificates are essential to felicitate the involvement of the participants leading to an accomplishment or successful completion.

Are certificates worth it? Is certification important?

Certificates are essential not only for resumé but also for continued skill development—certificates awarded for extensive work experience in a specific domain or accomplishments for completing a particular course.

Certificates are necessary and advantageous for a variety of reasons.

Certificates are essential and significant not only for the sake of your career but understandings also. And the fact that the value attached to your certificate will make it even more worthy.

Here are some quick points which are essential in terms of certificate designing. 

1) Components of a certificate


Over many years, certificates have been used as a credential to have met the professional standards and requirements to be capable enough to perform a particular task.

It includes the name of the person certified, the institution and its logo, the feat achieved and signature and the attesting authority, etc. 

2) Should look professional

Certificates are not just meant for starting a career or introduce yourself to a new field; they are needed to be achieved and acquired for your continuous professional development.

Career driven people don't need certificates to decorate their resume; they mean what they hold and attach to their resume. But carrying invaluable certifications that do not have any importance for the candidate or the organisation is absurd.

3) What certificate is made of?

A certificate will look fine between 300 GSM and 350 GSM in art paper.

Matte and glossy paper is used.

Parchment paper is commonly used for certificate printing.

4) Fonts and colours

Decorative fonts for the title and name of the recipient should be bold.

Illustrations and design patterns can be added for recipient satisfaction and acclamation.

Colours should be used in a way that the content is clear and legible. Bold colours may be used.
