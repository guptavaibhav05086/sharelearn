Do You Have a Perfect Label for Your Brand?

Stickers/Labels are used for various reasons. They are intelligible and can be used temporarily. They should be designed appropriately for onlookers' successful experience by guiding them towards their goals. They are mostly used for packaging and boxing.

Labelling enables brand promotion. Labels are unique and ensure product recognition. Labelling helps customers to make a choice based on visual appeal and brand popularity.

They aren't just used for branding and advertising but also used as warnings, instructions to use, etc. Labels can be in the form of: stickers, temporary or permanent labels and printed packaging.

Following are the steps to get personalized and professional-looking 'product labels'. It will not only save costs but also help to know your product better.  

1) What's your Label for


The product you are making the Label for needs to be well researched as to what kind of Label will be retained for long and what label material would be suitable for the product packaging. For example, bottles and other surfaces are exposed to extremities.

Types of labels: 

  • Brand Label - these labels only carry the name of the product.
  • Grade Label - grade labels tell the quality or grade of the product.
  • Descriptive Label - these emphasize on the complete details of the product.
    •  

The material used or the substrate the Label will be printed on: 

  • Paper: any kind of paper, hard or heavy and light or coated can be used.
  • Films or foils: thin strips which are made of metal or plastic.
  • Textiles: natural and synthetic fabrics are used to print labels.

2) Immediate call to action

Another way to derive the most from Labels is to start using them as a call to action practice. Print a coupon code or promotional deal onto a sticker and add it to a flyer to track your deal's response. Also, it can be an excellent way to highlight any promotional discounts you might have on in-store items, enticing consumers to buy the product or seek more details. 

3) Label design through right colour modes and picture resolution

Images on Labels should be of high resolution, so they don't look blurry when printed. High-resolution photos of at least 300 dpi should be used.

All colours are characteristic of the unique meaning they present. Like green represents the environment and good luck; orange shows warmth and energy. Picking the right colours is essential to convey the correct information of the product compatible with packaging.

In case of warning or instruction stickers/Labels, colours and details mentioned on them should be well visible and legible so that they can be read in the caliginosity.

4) Keep fonts in mind

Keep the information which is crucial to attend in legible and enlarged fonts. Add the unimportant details in the required space if available.

Appropriate font size, attractive offers on labels, other enticing celebrity pics and attention-grabbing colour schemes and patterns will always work.
